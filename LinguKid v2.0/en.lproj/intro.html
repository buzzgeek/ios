LinguKid is a simple learning tool to help small children become acquainted with English vocabulary. The concept is simple: friendly, inviting pictures are used interactively – just a touch on the screen and your child will hear the word which describes the object touched.

LinguKid offers your child basic knowledge of vocabulary in five subject areas – the home and household objects, animals, body parts, basics (numbers, letters, colors) and Christmas (new in version 1.1). Choose the subject area you wish to explore by selecting and then touching it on the opening scene. One touch is all you need to transport you to the colorful scene.


Once your child has explored the scene and become familiar with the English word for each object, a game mode may be selected in which your child will be asked where the object is. Your child can then test his or her knowledge by touching the appropriate object on the screen.

Once a scene has been selected, you have several options:

* Begin touching! The pictures are linked to the words which describe them. In some scenes only a few objects are active; on others every space in the scene is active. Touch once to hear and/or see the word. Have fun exploring!



* Double touch to enter a new scene. Some objects can be double touched to transport you to a new scene of related pictures and words. The words describing such objects will appear in green at the bottom of the screen and you will hear a distinguishing sound effect. Once in the new scene you can start learning all the new words.



*Choose the options menu. By touching the options menu symbol at the bottom right hand corner of the screen, a small menu will pop up. Here you can choose several options:

return to the opening screen



move into Q&A game mode



move into tile matching game mode



return to the previous scene



reduce the menu again

Q&A Game Mode. Let’s see if you can remember all the words to describe the objects! In game mode, you’ll be asked “Where is the…” followed by a random word describing one object in the scene. Just listen and touch the appropriate object.




If you choose correctly, you’ll get a smiley face in the upper left hand corner. If you guess incorrectly, you’ll get a sad face in the upper right hand corner.



Three smilies or three sad faces and the game is over. Try again if you like.

Tile Matching Game Mode. Let’s play a game of tile matching. First select the number of players to start the game. Select one player to play against the computer or two players to play against a friend.



To win the game you will have to find as many matching tiles as possible.

And that’s basically it!

Of course, you also have some options while running LinguKid. To find these options, just touch the settings button on the lower left hand corner of the opening screen. There you can choose how loud you want to set the music and spoken words. You can also choose if you want to see the words written out at the bottom of the screen or not.
